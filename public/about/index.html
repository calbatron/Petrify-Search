About

Set up by the BBC and Arts Council England, The Space is a commissioner of art that employs technology to push the boundaries of creative expression.

We support new talent and great artists from all art forms, creative industries, technical and digital backgrounds, through regular Open Calls, commissions and partnerships.

  How Does The Space Work?

The Space will showcase commissions of artists who employ new and existing technologies in their work and help nurture and develop projects on the cutting edge of the digital arts.

The Space is curated and programmed by a professional team and wants to develop new models of participation that allow everybody to contribute. It will feature a range of editorial content that explores the exciting world of digital art and how technology is increasingly informing a wide range of artistic practice.

The Space collaborates and co-commissions with art centres and galleries, festivals, broadcasters, digital partners and creative industries to give new opportunities to everybody, whether they are artists or audiences.

The Space is a Community Interest Company – that means it does not exist for profit, but has a public service aim to serve the interests of artists and audiences.

  Open Call

The Open Call is open to anybody in the world over the age of 18. We are looking for original, groundbreaking ideas that exist on the internet and can be experienced on mobile and tablet devices.

Successful ideas will be shortlisted and asked for more information via email. We will offer winners a paid commission, as well as an online platform to publish their work, and training and mentoring support to help develop their expertise.

Our second Open Call closed on 14 November 2014. There will be another Open Call in 2015.

The three Fellows chosen will receive £30,000 plus an additional £10,000 budget for training and mentorship working towards their proposed art projects.   Editorial Policy

The Space’s Editorial Policy applies to all the artworks it publishes.

As an on-demand service based in the UK, The Space is bound by ATVOD Statutory Rules and English law. It is not bound by the OFCOM Broadcasting Code but uses this as a reference in deciding what is appropriate for the service.

The guiding principles of The Space’s Editorial Policy are:

  • Content must comply with all applicable laws and regulation
  • Artistic freedom of expression will be respected and protected
  • The Space’s Equal Opportunities Policy and cultural difference and diversity will be respected
  • World class standards of quality and innovation should be the aim always and it is understood that this requires risk taking and trust

We will clearly signpost challenging content to ensure that our audiences have an appropriate expectation of our content and the opportunity to make informed choices about its suitability, both for themselves and for children, before they access it.

The Chief Executive is accountable for the implementation of the Editorial Policy.

  Accessibility Policy

The Space is where art meets everyone and everyone meets art and is therefore keen to ensure that the site and the works are accessible to everybody, as audiences, participants, artists and workers.

The Space is especially keen to ensure that world-class deaf and disabled artists have the opportunity to develop and show their work with and for The Space.

At a high level, our approach to accessibility is to make the website easy to use and understand for all visitors. This includes anyone who needs to or prefers to customise their devices so that they can access the website effectively. Our approach is to provide web pages with clear, easy to follow content and systems to work with. We recognise that it is not just disabled people who are affected by accessibility and, our strategy, plans and vision also include people:

  • Surfing the web using mobile or tablet devices
  • Not using the latest browsers or computers
  • Who are not confident using computers and require easy-to-use systems
  • Who have English as a second language
  • Who are interacting with The Space in a venue where they are unable to access sound as well as visuals

We welcome comment from audiences and artists about our site and suggestions about how we would improve our site and our approach to accessibility.

  Cookies Policy

What are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files used by websites to make your experience more efficient. European law states that we can store cookies on your device when they are essential to the operation of this site but that you need to consent to the storing of other cookies.

Essential Cookies

The Space uses a ‘PHP session cookie’, which remembers your actions between one web page and the next, for example to confirm that you have submitted a form. This cookie expires when you close your browser. By continuing to use this site you agree to this cookie being stored on your device.

Google Analytics Cookies

We use Google Analytics, a popular web analytics service provided by Google, Inc.

Google Analytics uses cookies to collect information about how visitors use our site. We use the information to compile reports and to help us improve the site. The cookies collect information in an anonymous form, including the number of visitors to the site, where visitors have come to the site from and the pages they visited.

We also use the Demographic and Interest Reporting that forms part of Google Analytics’ Advertising Features on our site. We do not display advertising to visitors to our site but we do use these reports to better understand and serve visitors to our website.

How Google’s Demographic and Interest Reporting Works

When someone visits a website that has partnered with the Google Display Network, Google stores a number in their browsers using a cookie to remember their visits. This number uniquely identifies a web browser on a specific computer, not a specific person. Browsers may be associated with certain interests or a demographic category, such as gender, age range, or parental status, based on the sites that were visited.

In addition, some sites provide Google with demographic or interest information that people share on sites such as social networking sites and Google makes use of demographics derived from Google profiles.

You can find out how to change settings relating to Google Analytics Advertising Features here 

If you do not wish analytics cookies to be stored on your device, you can opt out of Google cookies
